World Setting

Welcome to Xep, an Earth-like planet in a different time and place than our own; most of which is currently in a time similar to what we refer to as the “middle ages”. The lands here are divided into only two massive continents, the bigger of which is known as Focul. This incredibly diverse continent houses eleven of the thirteen nations found on Xep. These regions have long been torn by traditions of races keeping to themselves, and the ensuing violent struggles for personal territory that come with them. Many felt as though they were doomed to remain locked in this war perpetually, until that fateful day when something awful happened that proved itself a far worse threat than mere racial differences.

Without warning, a portal to the Destroyers Den; a hellish dimension that was built as a prison for unruly lifeforms and the enemies of the Pantheon, tore open and spilled forth a handful of beings one would find in their darkest nightmares. Overnight, the city of Snide was reduced to a smoldering, blood-soaked maze of lost lives and debris at the hands of a horde of demons and undead. They claimed the ruins for their own and gathered their strength behind the safety of a force field. However, they were without leadership thanks to a group of mercenaries nearly lost their lives in the defeat of the Fallen Angel now known as Harrow. It goes on to tell of how their lives were saved by a source of magic thought lost for nearly a decade; a rare gem called a Soul Orb, that can instantly restore the of life of a mortal being.

Ki-Zen, the oldest and most powerful mortal on the planet, personally felt the loss of each life taken that night. His heart was heavy with sadness as he watched over those he had hired to carry out a seemingly simple mission. While they survived thanks to the Soul Orbs, they were all badly  poisoned by an inky black ichor that was only countered after a steady stream of rare antitoxins over the course several days. Ki-Zen saw to it they were tended to by the finest doctors he could find, and they all made a full recovery. They were well compensated for their efforts, and offered a place among his new army.

These mortals earned the title of the Elite, and became the first members offered the chance to band together under his guidance in order to end this tragedy before it grew worse. Ki-Zen put forth a call to every kingdom and corner of Xep; a welcome to those of any race or social standing, that if they be strong of heart and capable of great deeds yet undone, may they step forward to aid in the reclamation of the world they call home. Some called this move an act of desperation, a plea for help falling upon deaf ears; but as fate would have it, many more recognized this as a chance for the childish behaviors of the past to be put aside. The cause grew great attention quickly, and an army unlike Xep had ever seen took shape before his very eyes.

By the following morning, the news had already reached the opposite side of the planet. On the continent of Londranis, a place said to be unreachable from Focul without the use of magic, the industrious nation of Cogland decided to finally share their knowledge and advanced technologies with the mainlands after nearly twenty years. Pocket watches, explosives, a variety of new age firearms, steam power, and Kurrent (electricity produced by ‘bottling’ lightning) were among the many inventions shared with all of Focul, along with the know how to properly use and reproduce them. These gifts were distributed to the leaders of every major nation and kingdom, but they were not well received by all. Some nations became uneasy with the realization that they had until now lacked such clearly superior weapons and defenses; while others paid them no heed to them at all. Either way, no one was simply ignoring the danger posed by the gathering army of undead and demons.

Little did all these brave mortals know that, like those responsible for the current calm before the storm, they would not be alone in their coming struggles. In fact, the Pantheon had taken notice of their efforts and for the first time in nearly a century they were humbled by the decisions of mortals choosing to fight back against forces unknown. The Balance, the harmony of both good and evil existing at once, was now in very real danger of favoring the evils of the world. If such a thing were to occur, it is said that endless darkness would befall the skies as they filled with smoke, the oceans and seas would boil away, and all life on Xep would be snuffed out as the violet flames continued to spread and consume everything in their path. To ensure this outcome is avoided, the Pantheon has promised to provide Ki-Zen with the resources he needs to have a chance in this war.

Another sunrise is nearly upon us, and it will be a more than just a new day. It will be a new era; a time when the ways of old are shaken at their core. The history of Xep is still being written, and now its in your hands! What say you? Will you stand and fight with Ki-Zen and the Elite? Or will you cower in your home and wait for the war to come to you?

Marshals

Also called an ST (storytellers), these are the referees of the game, usually stated at the beginning of each game. They will always wear something specific to designate them as such. What they say, goes. If they are wrong, it is up to another staff member to correct them, not the players. Doing so disrespectfully will eventually result in you being banned from playing the game altogether. If they make you play something out incorrectly they will be informed etc. Marshals caught cheating or treating players unfairly will be subject to losing their position and/or being asked to leave and not come back at all.

Medical Staff

Those with medical training may specify this to staff, in order to become eligible as a Health Marshal. We never wish for anyone to get hurt or injured, but accidents do happen. In such cases Health Marshals will be called forth to help provide for those in need. This position not only shows your character in real life, but earns you a small in game reward as well. They will have authority to remove a player from play if they feel they are injured or ill, or endangering themselves. Health Marshals will also be expected to assist with ensuring all players have water while on the trails as well. You are not in charge, you are just another line of defense against players becoming dehydrated or worse.

Cast

These are the players who are the main characters of the story, including yourself. Stories will generally revolve around those characters and the choices they make. These characters are referred to as PC’s, or Player Characters.. Of course there are countless people who also live in this world besides them, referred to as NPC’s. Without these hard working players you have no baddies to fight, no merchants to buy from, etc. They are here for the enjoyment of the PC’s, but don't forget, they want to have a good time too!

In Game & Out of Game Knowledge

Some things you will know regardless, but you cannot use out of game knowledge to give you an edge in the game. There are times where things will be “unseen”, “unheard”, “invisible” etc. These things are to be just that. While you are aware of them, your character is not! Using information acquired while not in character is a form of cheating.

Out of Genre Items

So, while the game encourages immersion, we never aim to deprive you of things that you need for medical reasons, or to remove all traces of the modern world from sight; thats just not feasible. What we can do is say, in the case of an inhaler or other medical supplies one may have, are more than welcome to be brought along, and not doing so would be the case in which there would be a problem. As for things like cell phones and the like, please leave them and all other non essential items with your out of game things. We are here to have a good time, not make you miserable!

Cheating & Offensive Behavior

There are many forms of this. Just dont do it. It isn't necessary, and it’s likely that the other players who have paid to have a good time are not going to be fond of your doing so. Everyone gets in the moment time and again, and we have all “fudged” some numbers time and again, it happens. This game is designed to give its players more than enough advantages. Repeat offenders will be banned from returning to future events, as we are all (hopefully) mature enough men and women that we can all play fair and still have fun.

Bottle To Blade Rule

To avoid injury and sickness at game, no one who has had alcohol in the last 6 hours will be allowed to play. Being caught under the influence of alcohol during game will result in your removal, and you may be asked not to return as well. This is a precaution that staff is more than comfortable in enforcing. We hope to never have to deal with this issue.

Character Packets

Players will each receive a packet with all the equipment, coin, etc. belonging to one of their characters at the beginning of each event. These packets are returned to staff at the end of each game. This is to ensure all things related to game are kept together and are accessible to staff for updating between games.

Armor Rating

Light Armor is heavier clothing or light leathers with minimal coverage.

Medium Armor is chainmail and thicker leathers that must cover your entire torso.

Heavy Armor should appear heavy and must cover two thirds of your body.

The Grade System

Many abilities in this book mention in their description that they may be purchased multiple times, and others do not. As a rule of thumb, if it states it can be purchased more than once, it can be. If not, you should assume it can only be purchased once.

Discount Stacking

There are certainly various ways to reduce the cost of your XP expenditures, and if you possess more than one that would reduce the cost of the same purchase, each of them apply at once, unless noted. This is to ensure that certain builds will learn specific abilities much faster than others, as they should. Purchased abilities have a mandatory base cost of at least 1 XP. That makes sure your character does not simply receive new powers without having tried at least trying to learn them.

Ammunition

If you carry a firearm, you must have ammunition for it as well. Each “Ammunition’ item card you possess allows you to carry a stated number of rounds; coming in increments of 10, 20, and 30. These item cards stack of course. Expended rounds cannot be retrieved until the end of the encounter in which they are fired. Be sure to write your characters name on each dart to be sure it is returned to its proper place.

COMBAT

The fighting in this system occurs with contact friendly weapons and tactics, allowing the players to engage one another without the constant threat of injury. You must actually say the full damage call from a single attack before swinging again with that weapon, and after three unanswered attacks to your opponent, you must wait at least one second before engaging them again. Blows that are physically or mechanically parried, resisted, etc. do not count towards the three swing “limit”.

Ability Refreshes (Arcs)

Every 8 Hours of game is known as an Arc. At the beginning of each Arc, all your abilities that are limited to a certain number of uses per Arc become immediately available again! If you gain a certain number of Mana at the beginning of an Arc, that refreshes as well.

The Definition of an Encounter

An encounter, is defined as the period of time between when an NPC first appears, to when they have all gone. As an example, if the PCs are walking down a trail and they meet a guard standing at his post, you are officially in an encounter. Until it is just the PC’s and they have been told they have the trail, it is considered the same encounter. You will not always have combat during an encounter.

Non-Combatant

These people have come to play the game but do not wish to involved in the combat portion of the game. They are still welcome on missions, but will always be assumed to have been overwhelmed or captured alive, etc. if the other characters are all downed. These players cannot carry weapons and they are never to be physically attacked in any manner, save touching them with your weapon and calling damage once every other second that you maintain contact with the target.

Brothers Keeper

If a player is about to do something unsafe, call Brothers Keeper and help them avoid it. This is a legitimate way to keep you and your fellow players from stepping in a hole, tripping over fallen tree limbs, standing on an anthill, or any number of other unforeseen hazards that can be caused by running around like a bunch of crazy people in the woods!

Drop Rule

You cannot use bows with strings against targets within 10 feet. You cannot move into drop range but if they move into your range on their own you can drop the readied shot to the ground and call your damage as a pointcast. Bow weapons should have no more than a 20 pound draw if they are real bows. You can use approved toy bows. You can also use packet archery instead, and do not suffer this ruling, but must count to 5 and pull out a packet to throw in place of an arrow.

Player VS Player

Players are held accountable for their actions, but this does not stop them from causing each other personal harm. However, there is an exception to this rule. While on the sacred grounds of Frostlight, no one can be killed due to the powerful divine magics that protect the fortress and all its inhabitants. You are not invincible, as you still experience all forms of pain and suffering, but you cannot drop below 1 HP. This blessing allows for those in training to employ lethal weapons without the worry of accidental casualties. There are also plenty of talented healers and physicians under the employ of the Vanguard.

Healing

When you lose HP there are a few different ways to get it back, and cheat death yet again. There are alchemical potions that restore hit points through combining and brewing natural herbs and other ingredients. Another method is physical medicine, involving the use of a Vita-Pack to close the most grievous of wounds. Last but far from least, there is magic; this is often the quickest way to close a wound and get someone back in the fight.

Dying

If your HP reaches zero, you remain conscious, but you may not continue to fight. You must take a knee or otherwise indicate you have been ‘dropped’. At this time you begin a 120 count (2 minutes). While being Stabilized or Healed, halt your count but do not lose track of your timer, as you must start from there again if they are not successful. If you are not stabilized or healed for at least 1 HP in that time you will die. However, this isnt a problem if you have a Soul Orb; the very life essence of another being, trapped within a small red crystal. It may sound morbid, but you will certainly be glad you have it one day. Each PC is gifted one of these items at character generation!

Loot

When you come upon a downed NPC, you can search them for items, coin, and other various . by completing a full 10 second count. You may not loot fellow PC’s.

Currency

Silver Piece - Once this was the only denomination of coin. 5 Silver is equal 1 Gold.

Gold Piece - Once a rare thing to see, the Gold piece is now a common denomination used on Xep. It is said that the God Midan made and distributed these himself.

Item Cards

These are a means of giving players weapons, armor, potions and other various things within the in game world, without having to have the actual physical item. Item cards should have a phys-rep if possible. You may not use things such as weapons without having both, the proper item card and phys-rep.

Safety Specifications

Each weapon will be inspected at the start of each game to ensure they have been maintained between games. If a weapon is deemed unsafe, it can be fixed (if possible) and then inspected. This is an important step towards the safety of each of our players.

Melee weapons will be checked for safety at each game. There are many acceptable latex weapon brands out there, but home-made weapons are acceptable as well, should they follow safety guidelines.

Firearm phys-reps must be approved. There are lots of acceptable choices, but the one you pick must be single fire only, nothing automatic. No ‘slamfire’ can be used! You must provide your own darts, and you should write your characters name on each of them if you would like them returned. ORANGE TIPS MUST REMAIN VISIBLE, even if you paint the remainder of the weapon! This is to indicate the gun is only a toy, and it is not optional.

Bow phys-reps that are not Nerf guns can have no more than a 25 pound draw. Crossbows should only be firing foam darts.

Arrow phys-reps can be purchased online at many retailers. All arrows will be CLOSELY monitored for safety and wear. You may also use packet archery if you wish.

Packets must be at least the size of an eye socket, and should be made of cloth and filled with only biodegradable materials in case it is lost. Foam stress balls of certain types can also be used if they are approved by staff.

Unique Weapons

Some weapons do not fit with any of the above items. Any such weapon can be submitted to staff to be examined for safety and aesthetics. If staff believes the weapon to be a worthwhile addition to the game, it will be given stats and allowed into the game.

Weapon Lengths

Descriptors

Damage types matter in the overall flow of combat. Some beings will resist, reduce, or even be immune to certain damage types; while others will be forced to take the entirety of that same damage should the attack land successfully.

Basic Damage

This type of damage is called as just a number, which indicates the amount of damage the attack or effect is inflicting. We as staff just did not feel the need to enforce descriptors for these attacks beyond the damage itself. If for some reason the need to add them arises, we will deal it will. Until then, we shall try this system.

Nonbasic Damage

This type of damage is most commonly inflicted by magic, and the often hostile wildlife forms that now inhabit the various environments found on Xep.

Air - Focused bursts of wind that become concussive physical attacks.  

Acid - Corrosive fluids that are capable of dissolving most any material on the planet.

Earth - The abrasive bits and pieces of nature. Sticks and stones can break bones.

Fire - Damage caused by heat or flames. One of the most offensive schools of magic.

Holy - Pure white ‘light’ that can do anything from heal wounds, to vaporizing the Unholy.

Lightning - Natural electricity, focused into bolts of bright, crackling pain.

Magic - Raw magical energies, concentrated into a physical form.

Unholy - Described as a black ‘light’ used most often used for pure destruction.

Water - Water is an element of life, and an all too often underestimated power source.

Critical Damage

Once inflicted, this type of damage cannot be avoided without the use of a Phase, or an Immunity to the declared type (if any). The Critical descriptor is sometimes added to attacks or effects. The following types are always considered to be Critical, unless otherwise noted.

Life Drain - Part of your life force is ripped away, healing its new host in the process.

Poison - Poison damage may not be healed without employing certain items or abilities.

Status Effects

 
Physical Effects

Bind Limb - Declared limb is locked in place. If a leg is affected, you suffer a Frozen Foot for the duration. If an arm is affected you suffer the disarm effect to that hand.

Blind - You only deal only 1 damage with all attacks and you cannot use pointcast or melee range pointcast abilities for the duration.

Disarm - Targeted item must be thrown aside and cannot be picked up for 10 seconds.

Frozen Foot - You must choose a leg and leave in in place for the remainder of the effect. You may walk in a small circle as long as the affected leg remains in contact with that spot. Leaning far enough to cause your heel to rise is considered bad form.

Knockback - You are thrown in a straight line 5 paces, unless otherwise stated. This should be done in a timely manner, as you are not a valid target while taking these steps. Do not abuse this in order to avoid combat, that is a form of cheating.

Paralyze - Your muscles seize up, rendering you unable to move, speak, or activate abilities not already in effect for the duration. You may still use resists during this time.

Sleep - You must drop your hands to your sides and pretend to be in a deep sleep for the duration. You immediately wake up if you are damaged before the end of this time.

Slow Motion - You may only move and fight at a painfully slow speed for the duration. If a staff member asks you to move slower, please do so. All cast times are tripled.

Smash Limb - Affected limb is useless until repaired. If a leg is affected you suffer the Snare effect for the duration. If an arm is affected you suffer a Disarm effect to that arm, and it must remain at your side.

Snare - You may only move at a slow walk for the duration. You still fight at normal speed.

Stun - You can only defend yourself, suffer frozen foot on a single leg, and cannot cast spells or activate abilities not already in use for the duration.

Weakness - You cannot use Strength or Feat of Strength, deal only base weapon damage, and any Mana you possess becomes inaccessible for the duration.

Mental Effects

Dominate - You will obey basic commands from the user, such as “take that man's gold“, “defend me” or “get me some water”. Being told to do something such as kill a loved one will likely end the effect instantly. You retain the knowledge of what you have done. Higher forms of this effect may not follow these rules.

Rage - You are overwhelmed by anger and must meaningfully attack the target or targets nearest to you, whether enemy or ally. You will still attack foes over allies if both are present.

Fear - You must immediately move out of melee range of the user, and may only defend yourself against that target for the duration.

Lure - You must move towards the users current location before taking any other actions. Once there you may still fully engage the user and other targets you can reach; but while under this effect, if the user moves away, you must move with them if possible.

Pacify - You relax and cannot feel any form of anger or make aggressive actions for the duration, even if damaged or otherwise harmed.

Taunt - You must meaningfully attempt to engage the user in combat immediately, ignoring all other targets for the duration. While you cannot make offensive actions against others, you can still dodge and resist outside attacks if possible, and still inflict normal damage to other targets around the user with area effects.

Terror - You must immediately move out of melee range of all foes and may only defend yourself against them for the duration.

General Terms

Armor Resist - Used to Resist 10 or less damage and any partnered status effects, from a single source. This ability is player activated, allowing them to choose when it is used. These cannot be used to stop Critical damage or a status effect that is not paired with damage. These must be activated individually, and cannot be stacked.

Blast - This is to indicate that an attack, ability, or spell deals area damage. If delivered by dart, arrow, or packet this targets its final resting place. If delivered by Pointcast or melee range pointcast, this affects the area around the target. Unless stated, this distance is roughly melee range.

Burst - This indicates an attack, ability, or spell is dealing its damage and effects to a large area with you at its center. Unless stated, this distance is roughly melee range.

Come Along - This is to simulate carrying another person. A player with the Strength ability can carry two other people, moving at Snared speed. Those without this ability may only carry one. While using this ability you must remain in contact with the target(s).

Convert - Change a damage type from one to another for a stated period of time.

Death - You immediately drop to zero HP and go into Bleed Out.

Defense - Each point you possess reduces all damage received by 1, to a minimum of 1.

Dodge - Your character avoids damage that you as a player did not. This can be used to avoid Critical damage, but cannot be used to avoid Bursts, Blasts, or abilities dealt by voice cast. You cannot impose yourself in front of another person and then use this ability.

Enhancement - These effects add damage or the like to attack to increase their effectiveness for a short time. Only one effect of this type may be applied at a time.

Heal - You immediately gain the specified number of hit points.

Hit Points (HP) - These represent a character's capacity to remain on their feet when taking a beating. Every race has a base HP, gaining more based on Class, and abilities. If your HP is ever at zero, you are in Bleed Out.

Melee Range Pointcast - You successfully hit a target within roughly two feet of you.

Mindless - This being is Immune to Mental status effects.

Minimal - The attack only dealt 1 damage to the target, due to some type of resistance.

Out of Game - The affected item or player is not able to interact with the in game world for the duration. For this time period they simply do not exist.

Pointcast - You successfully hit a target within line of sight, no more than 20 paces away.

Phase - This is used to avoid any type of damage or effect, including Critical damage.

Immune - The target took no damage or partnered status effects from your last attack or action, and it will continue to have no effect.

Reflect - The person who designated you as a target takes whatever call they just made against you. You take nothing!

Resist - You negate a single attack or effect of a specified type, taking no damage or status effects from it. Most often these cannot be used to stop Critical damage.

Stabilize - You are no longer in Bleed Out, but you remain unconscious at 0 HP. If you are not Healed within 5 minutes, you wake up at 1 HP.

Sunder - Affected weapon can only deal base damage for that weapon type until it is repaired. If the item suffers this effect again before being repaired, it is ‘broken’ and useless in combat. If this effect strikes a natural weapon, it becomes a Smash Limb instead.

Vulnerable - You take Critical damage from the stated descriptor for the duration of the effect. If you are Immune to the stated type, that Immunity is suppressed during this time.

Battlefield Conditions

These conditions are imposed by the environment the players are currently occupying. Fighting amidst a powerful storm or inside a burning vessel presents deadly hazards to all those who are present. These conditions will always be clearly announced by an ST.

Ablaze - Affected players will be informed by an ST to take 3 Fire damage every 10 seconds, until the condition is removed.

Ambush - Affected players are caught off guard by foes, and suffer a Stun 20.

Blizzard - Affected players move and fight at half speed, and will be informed by an ST to take 3 Water damage every 10 seconds.

Darkness - Affected players suffer the Blindness effect for the duration.

Drowning - Affected players cannot breathe, speak, or cast spells. They will be informed by an ST to take 10 Critical damage every 10 seconds, until they can breathe again.

Multi-Monster - This is a foe represented by more than one NPC, in order to make up for the size difference. Depending on this size of this enemy, the PC’s may be informed they are all under the Snared condition. Any such condition will be announced.

Overgrowth - Affected players suffer the Snare effect.

Sandstorm - Affected players suffer the Blindness effect, and will be informed by an ST to take 3 Earth damage every 10 seconds.

Water Pressure - Affected players cannot move, speak, or cast spells. They will be informed by an ST to take 10 Critical damage every 10 seconds, until they can move again.

CREATING A CHARACTER

Now that you are familiar with the world setting and the basic rules, you are ready to start making a character of your very own. All you will need is pen, paper, and this rulebook! Lets start off by covering something that seems to plague many gamers: MATH!

The Min/Max Struggle

Want to get the most out of your character mechanically speaking? Thinking about running all the numbers to see what build affords the most damage where? We all do it. Then please read the following first, as it is intended to serve as a helper in easing that process. This chart does not account for damage related to natural weapons, or Devotion.

Melee & Ranged

One-handed weapons deal a base of 2 damage.

Two-handed weapons deal a base of 4 damage.

Firearms, two-handed bows, and crossbows deal a base of 8 Critical damage.

Thrown Weapons deal a base of 2 damage.

A single grade of Weapon Mastery adds +1 damage with the chosen weapon.

A second grade of Weapon Mastery adds +2 (total) damage with the chosen weapon.

Magic

A Mage Bolt deals 4 damage, but costs no Mana to cast.

Basic Spells deal a base of 8 damage, and cost 1 Mana to cast.

Intermediate Spells deal a base of 10 damage, and cost 2 Mana to cast.

Master Spells deal a base of 13 damage, and Cost 3 Mana to cast.

A single grade of Spell Mastery grants +1 damage to all damaging spells you cast.

A second grade of Spell Mastery grants +2 (total) damage to all damaging spells you cast.

BOTTOM LINE: JUST PLAY A CHARACTER THAT LETS YOU HAVE FUN!

Character Sheet

Choose a background, a race, and a class, then spend your starting experience points on whatever you have access to in order to mold your character into what you want him or her to be. All this information is compiled on your character sheet as quick reference. Three Game Rule

I understand that when choosing a character in a new system, that you may end up not liking something as much as you thought you would, for a multitude of reasons. You have three games to decide what you want to play, when you PC your fourth game, that character sheet is locked in. All changes made to it before that are inconsequential.

Character History

This is nation where you grew up, and the place where you have the strongest ties. Each has its own set of advantages that relate to the common lifestyle of those who live there.

Blight

Originally founded as the farming community Kangker, named after the family who first settled here, these lands were said to be rich and fertile, but are instead harsh, and full of salty deposits from the sea. As time passed and crops withered or failed to grow altogether, it become known as Blight, and has since been referred to as such by most. Once it at least appeared to be a suitable place to settle down, though currently it’s nothing more than a disheveled arrangement of farm houses and mud. Several years ago, plague wrought this town of its weakest members; a result of poor hygiene and a lack of doctors. Most of the towns religious figures have long left. No more than a few hundred people remain in these barren lands, but those who remain have have become almost mute to the health problems that they faced in the past.

Cogland is an island continent of forest covered mountains and industrial colonies. The biggest and most populated of the three is called The Steam Sector. Brilliant minds here have harnessed steam to power all sorts of mechanical devices that now comprise a city that always seems to be on the move; after all, this is where a still unknown individual successfully developed firearms and other weaponized explosives. The second largest of the colonies is known as the Blue Quarter, a place that welcomes those with coin to spend. It got its name from the local method of power that created its signature black and blue atmosphere. The region closest to the area that was destroyed by the removal of a tremendous portion of land (when Nimbuss left, is known as Flicker, and its the only portion of the island that has unreliable electricity; largely due to the lack of people willing or able to feed the coal burners all day. Pantheon

The Balance

In this world, there are many Gods and Goddesses who literally walk among the common folk they so resemble. In truth they are immortals, each possessing exceptional and seemingly infinite power. They each have many followers who believe in their ways, and use it as a guideline to live their lives with. The truly devoted, known as Clerics, spend most of their waking lives working as the face and voice of their chosen deity, earning them in return godly powers to aid them in their cause. However, this power can be taken away far easier than it is given, as the one they serve is always watching, ready to remove their gifts should they witness behavior contrary to their pleasing. The ultimate goal of the Pantheon is maintaining the balance of good and evil, allowing both to exist in what peace they are able to make for themselves; though on more than one occasion in the history books, there are stories of personal intervention. It may seem hard to believe, but even the God of Death plays his role in maintaining this balance. There are indeed other Gods and Goddesses in existence, beyond those in the Pantheon. These deities have far less worshippers, and they are known to be outright evil. Men and women who are devoted to them keep this fact a secret to all but those who also praise their master. These deities seek to destroy the balance, and usurp the unmatched power of their kind to transform existence into a case of “survival of the fittest“.

Grimoire

Spells are a way of attacking using more than a mere weapon. They are the “bread and butter” of some characters, giving them the advantage of more than mortal steel over the hordes of evil they face on their adventures.

Casting Spells

To cast a spell you simply spend the proper amount of Mana and begin your cast time aloud, counting: 1 one thousand, 2 one thousand, or any other method of counting that keeps you from speed casting. Speed casting is essentially cheating, so just dont do that. Hand gestures, spell books, and other props, etc that add flair to this process are more than okay, they are encouraged! Those who go above and beyond will be rewarded for it. While casting you cannot attack foes or activate any other abilities, but you may still move freely and defend yourself. If you are damaged while casting you take damage as normal, but must restart your cast time. You do not lose Mana for starting over, but you will lose it if you declare an attack or activate any other offensive spell or ability. To cast a spell you must have at least one free hand to hold the packet or point at your target.

Purchasing Spells

You do not have to purchase spells in the order they appear, but you may never have more higher level spells than you do lower level spells. Example, if you only possess three Fire spells, you cannot possess more than three Intermediate and three Master spells within that school of sorcery.

40 SP - Golden Soul Orb - This instantly can instantly revive you from death at full HP.
